ArtGDance: Music-Driven 3D Group Dance Generation Framework

Anonymous Authors

Music-to-dance generation maps a music sequence to synchronized dance motions aligned with its rhythm and emotion.

Abstract

Group dance, as a complex and collaborative art form, requires performers to execute synchronized movements while maintaining dynamic spatial formations. Central to this art form is its inseparable connection with music, where rhythm, tempo, and emotional content guide bodily motion, creating a harmonious interplay between auditory and visual modalities. In this work, we address the challenges of music-driven group dance generation, a task that synthesizes natural, coherent, and synchronized group dance movements from music. To tackle these challenges, we propose the \textbf{ArtGDance framework}, which integrates a Music Encoder, a Dance Decoder, and a Dance Discriminator. These components are enhanced by Generative Adversarial Training (GAT) and a Kinetic Loss to significantly improve the naturalness, synchronization, and diversity of generated dance sequences. Additionally, we introduce a Retrieval model, composed of a Music Encoder, Dance Encoder, and training by contrastive learning. Based on this, we construct an evaluation framework to comprehensively assess the quality of the generated dances. Extensive experiments conducted on the AIOZ-GDANCE dataset demonstrate that ArtGDance achieves state-of-the-art performance, while advancing AI for Art.
